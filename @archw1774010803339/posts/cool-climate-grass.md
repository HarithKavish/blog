# Best Grass Types for Cool Climates

If you live in northern regions or at higher elevations, choosing the right cool-season grass can make the difference between a lush lawn and a patchy yard.

## Top Cool-Season Grasses

### Kentucky Bluegrass
- **Texture**: Fine to medium
- **Color**: Deep green
- **Spread**: Rhizomes (self-repairing)
- **Mowing Height**: 2.5–3 in (6–7.5 cm)
- **Notes**: Tolerates cold winters and moderate foot traffic

### Perennial Ryegrass
- **Germination**: Fast (5–7 days)
- **Texture**: Fine
- **Color**: Bright green
- **Use**: Often mixed with Kentucky bluegrass for quick cover
- **Mowing Height**: 2–2.5 in (5–6 cm)

### Tall Fescue
- **Texture**: Coarse to medium
- **Roots**: Deep (drought-tolerant)
- **Color**: Dark green
- **Heat Tolerance**: Better than most cool-season types
- **Mowing Height**: 3–4 in (7.5–10 cm)

## Planting Tips

1. **Timing**: Late summer/early fall gives the longest establishment period before winter.
2. **Seeding Rate**: 3–4 lb per 1,000 ft² (15–20 g/m²) for new lawns.
3. **Watering**: Keep top ½ in (1 cm) moist until seedlings reach 2 in (5 cm).

## Winter Care

- **Final Mow**: Cut slightly shorter (2 in) before the first hard frost to reduce snow mold.
- **Fertilizer**: Apply a winterizer (high potassium) in late fall.

Choose a single species or blend two for the best balance of color, durability, and disease resistance.
