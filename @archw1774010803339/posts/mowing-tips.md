# Lawn Mowing Tips for Healthier Turf

Mowing isn’t just a chore—it’s the most important cultural practice for a healthy lawn. Do it right and you encourage dense, deep-rooted grass that crowds out weeds.

## 1. Follow the One-Third Rule

Never remove more than one-third of the blade length in a single mow. Scalping stresses grass, reduces photosynthesis, and invites weeds.

## 2. Keep Blades Sharp

Dull blades tear grass, leaving ragged tips that turn brown and invite disease. Sharpen rotary blades every 20–25 hours of use.

## 3. Mow High

Taller grass shades soil, conserving moisture and blocking weed seeds:
- **Cool-season grasses**: 2.5–4 in (6–10 cm)
- **Warm-season grasses**: 1–3 in (2.5–7.5 cm)

## 4. Vary Your Pattern

Alternate direction each mow—north-south one week, east-west the next—to prevent soil compaction and rutting.

## 5. Leave Clippings (When Possible)

Grass clippings are 80–85 % water and decompose quickly, returning nitrogen to the soil. Skip bagging unless the lawn is diseased or clumps are excessive.

## 6. Mow Dry Grass

Wet blades bend and tear instead of cutting cleanly, plus clippings clump and smother turf.

## 7. Seasonal Adjustments

- **Spring**: Gradually lower height after the first green-up mow.
- **Summer**: Raise height ½ in (1 cm) to shade roots and reduce heat stress.
- **Fall**: Lower height slightly for the final cut to discourage snow mold.

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] Blade sharpened
- [ ] Deck height adjusted
- [ ] Fuel or battery charged
- [ ] Pattern planned

Mow smart and your grass will reward you with thicker, greener turf all season long.
