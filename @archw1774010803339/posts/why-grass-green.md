# Why Grass is Green: Chlorophyll Explained

Ever looked across a lawn and wondered why grass glows green? The answer is chlorophyll, the pigment that powers photosynthesis.

## What is Chlorophyll?

Chlorophyll is a molecule that absorbs red and blue wavelengths of sunlight and reflects green light, giving plants their characteristic color. It’s housed inside tiny organelles called chloroplasts, which are concentrated in the blades of grass.

## How Photosynthesis Works

1. **Light Absorption**: Chlorophyll captures energy from sunlight.
2. **Water Splitting**: The energy splits water molecules (H₂O) into hydrogen and oxygen.
3. **CO₂ Uptake**: Grass absorbs carbon dioxide from the air through tiny pores (stomata).
4. **Sugar Production**: The hydrogen combines with CO₂ to form glucose, which fuels plant growth.
5. **Oxygen Release**: Oxygen is released as a byproduct—fresh air for us to breathe.

## Fun Fact

Grass blades are thin and upright to maximize sunlight exposure, making them incredibly efficient solar panels.

Next time you walk barefoot across your lawn, remember: every blade is a tiny green factory converting sunlight into energy.
