Top 10 Grass Care Tips

  1. Mow High: Keep blades 2.5–3.5 inches to shade roots and crowd out weeds.
  2. Sharpen Mower Blades: Clean cuts reduce disease risk and water loss.
  3. Water Deeply but Rarely: 1 inch once or twice a week, early morning to minimize evaporation.
  4. Feed in Fall: A high-potassium fertilizer strengthens roots for winter.
  5. Aerate Compacted Soil: Pull plugs every 1–2 years so air, water, and nutrients reach roots.
  6. Overseed Thin Areas: Scatter matching seed after mowing short and raking lightly.
  7. Leave Clippings: They return nitrogen and organic matter to the soil.
  8. Control Weeds Early: Spot-treat broadleaf weeds before they flower and spread.
  9. Test Soil pH: Aim for 6.0–7.0; lime if acidic, sulfur if alkaline.
  10. Stay off Frozen or Wet Grass: Prevent crown damage and soil compaction.
